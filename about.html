THE PROJECT

Why We Built LagmaBills

A school project that turned into a real rescue robot — built for Arduino Day 2025 with civil protection in mind.

THE IDEA

One Robot,
Three Ways to Help

LagmaBills started as a class project at IIS Galileo Galilei in Crema, built around a simple question: in an emergency, what does a small team of first responders actually need eyes and hands for? The answer became three linked agents in one platform — a ground rover that can reach a debris field, a robotic arm that can move what people shouldn't have to, and a drone that can see what the rover can't. "Land, Ground & Midair Agent" isn't just a tagline, it's the literal division of labour.

DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Built to Work
Without the Internet

Real emergency scenes don't come with reliable WiFi. That's why every brain LagmaBills needs — navigation, voice AI, mapping — runs locally on the Raspberry Pi and the two onboard ESP32s. The only thing that ever leaves the robot is a Tailscale link back to the operator, and even that's optional: the robot can navigate and avoid obstacles entirely on its own.
